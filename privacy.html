Privacy notice

How we handle your information.

We keep this simple. We only collect what we need, we don’t sell your data, and we treat business submissions as confidential.

Minimal data
No selling
Common sense

Who we are

For GDPR purposes, Nelson Lord Partners is the “data controller” for information you submit through this website. If you need to contact us about privacy, email: info@nelsonlordpartners.co.uk.

What we collect

  • Contact details (name, email, sometimes phone)
  • Information you choose to share about a business (basic facts, notes, timing)
  • Any message you send us

Why we collect it

  • To respond to your enquiry or introduction
  • To decide whether there’s a fit for a confidential conversation
  • To keep a record of our communications

Our legal basis (in plain English)

Usually it’s one of these:
Legitimate interests — it’s reasonable for us to use your details to respond to you and assess fit.
Contract steps — if you’re asking us to discuss a potential transaction, we need details to progress that.
Consent — where you explicitly ask for something (e.g., “please send me…”).

How forms work

Our website forms are processed via Formspree (a form-handling service), so submissions are delivered to us. Formspree acts as a service provider/processor for the form data.

If you prefer not to use the form, you can email us directly.

How long we keep information

We keep information only as long as it’s useful and reasonable — for example, to continue a conversation, to revisit later if you ask us to, or to meet basic record-keeping needs. If you want us to delete something, ask — and we’ll handle it sensibly unless we have a genuine reason not to.

Your rights

  • Ask what we hold about you
  • Ask us to correct it
  • Ask us to delete it (in many cases)
  • Ask us to stop using it in certain ways
  • Ask for a copy of it

Email: info@nelsonlordpartners.co.uk.

Cookies / analytics

At the moment this site does not need cookies to function. If we add analytics later, we’ll do it transparently and keep it minimal.
