Confidentiality and process

Confidentiality is non-negotiable.

Owners often worry about leaks, gossip, or disruption. We understand that. We keep things private and controlled.

Our commitments
  • No contact with staff, customers, or suppliers without owner approval
  • Information shared on a need-to-know basis
  • Specialists involved only when appropriate
  • Clear steps and a calm timetable
  • Minimal disruption throughout
The aim

Keep the business steady while decisions are made.

Calm execution beats noise.

A simple four-step process

We keep it proportionate. We avoid unnecessary heaviness.

1
Confidential 15-minute owner call
Fit, priorities, and whether it is worth a second conversation.
2
Light review
Accounts and a few basics, only after genuine fit.
3
Meet properly and plan the transition
A deeper conversation about what matters, how to keep the team steady, and what the first months could look like.
4
Confirm and complete, with care
Confirmatory diligence that is proportionate, then a completion plan designed to avoid disruption.
Straight answer

We will not create disruption for the sake of it.

If it becomes heavy or noisy, it is usually a sign something is not right.
